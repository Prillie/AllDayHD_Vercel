Privacy Policy

How we handle your personal data and protect your privacy.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2025

All Day HD ("we", "us", "our") is an educational website focused on ADHD in girls and teens. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect personal data when you use this website.

1. Who is responsible for your data?

The data controller for this website is All Day HD. If you have questions about this policy or your data, you can contact us via the form on Contact Us.

2. What data we collect

  • Newsletter sign-up: name, email address, and your stated ADHD challenge (optional)
  • Contact form: name, email address, and any information you choose to include in your message
  • Technical data: basic technical information provided by your browser (e.g., IP address, browser type) as part of normal web server logs

3. How we use your data

  • Newsletter: to send you email updates, focus tips, and resources you have opted in to receive
  • Contact form: to respond to your questions or requests
  • Site safety and performance: to keep the website secure and functioning correctly

4. Legal bases for processing (GDPR)

Under the EU/UK GDPR we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Consent – for sending marketing emails and newsletters
  • Legitimate interests – for keeping the site secure and understanding basic usage (without profiling)

5. Email provider (Brevo)

We use Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) as our email service provider. When you subscribe to the newsletter, your name, email address, and optional "biggest ADHD challenge" are stored in Brevo so we can send you emails. Brevo acts as our data processor and processes your data on our behalf in accordance with GDPR.

6. Cookies and similar technologies

At this time we do not use analytics or advertising cookies on this site. We use only:

  • Essential cookies / storage – for example, sessionStorage used by the on-page focus timer so it remembers your session while you browse
  • Google Fonts – fonts are loaded from Google Fonts, which may receive your IP address as part of serving the font files

If we introduce analytics or advertising cookies in the future, we will update this policy and request your consent before setting them.

7. Data retention

  • Newsletter data: kept until you unsubscribe or we delete the list
  • Contact form data: kept for as long as needed to respond and for a limited period afterwards for record-keeping
  • Server logs: kept for a short period for security and troubleshooting

8. Your rights (EU / UK GDPR)

If you are in the EU or UK, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Withdraw consent at any time (for example, by unsubscribing from emails)
  • Object to certain types of processing

To exercise these rights, contact us via the Contact Us page or reply to any email we send.

9. Children's privacy

This site is designed to be used by parents/caregivers and teens. Newsletter sign-ups and direct contact should be submitted by individuals aged 13+ or by a parent/caregiver on behalf of a child. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13 without parental involvement.

10. Data security

We use HTTPS and industry-standard technical measures to protect your data. However, no method of transmission over the internet is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top will always show the current version. If we make significant changes, we will post a notice on the website.
