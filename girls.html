Things That Feel Hard (And That's OK!) üíô

Everyone has things that are tricky ‚Äî here are some common ones for ADHD brains

ü™ë Sitting Still

Your body wants to MOVE! Sitting at a desk for a long time can feel impossible. Your legs bounce, your hands fidget, and you just want to jump up!

Try: Fidget toys, standing desk, or wiggle cushion. Ask your teacher if you can stand while working sometimes!

‚è∞ Waiting Your Turn

When you have something to say, it feels like it might burst out of you! Waiting to be called on is SO hard. Sometimes you interrupt without meaning to.

Try: Drawing while you wait, or squeezing a stress ball. It helps your body be patient!

üß† Remembering Stuff

You might forget your homework, lose your jacket, or forget what you were supposed to do. Your brain is so busy with other thoughts!

Try: Checklists with pictures, putting things in the same spot every time, or using fun reminder alarms on a phone.

üò¢ When Feelings Get BIG

Sometimes feelings feel HUGE ‚Äî like your mad is REALLY mad, or your sad is SUPER sad. It can feel like your emotions have a volume knob stuck on HIGH.

Try: Taking deep breaths, going to a quiet space, or doing a quick dance break to let the big feeling out!

üë≠ Making Friends Who Understand

Sometimes other kids don't understand why you interrupt, fidget, or have so much energy. Finding friends who "get you" can take time.

Try: Look for other energetic, creative kids! Join clubs or activities where being enthusiastic is celebrated.

Cool Tools Just For You! üõ†Ô∏è

These tools help your ADHD brain work even better!

Visual Timers

These are timers you can SEE! They show you how much time is left in a colorful way, so you're not surprised when time is up.

Try these:
  • Time Timer (shows red disappearing)
  • Sand timers (fun to watch!)
  • Kitchen timer shaped like animals
  • Phone apps with animations
üòä

Feelings Chart

A chart with faces showing different feelings! Point to how you feel when words are hard to find. This helps grownups understand what you need.

How it helps:
  • Learn to name your feelings
  • Show others how you feel
  • Track patterns (sad on Mondays?)
  • Ask for what you need
üìã

Morning Routine Chart

Pictures showing each step of getting ready! No more forgetting to brush teeth or pack your backpack.

Your chart might show:
  • Wake up & get dressed
  • Brush teeth & wash face
  • Eat breakfast
  • Pack backpack & shoes on
üéØ

Fidget Toys

These are toys that help your hands stay busy so your brain can focus! Squish, twist, or spin them while you listen or work.

Popular fidgets:
  • Stress balls (squish!)
  • Fidget spinners or cubes
  • Silly putty or slime
  • Tangles or pop-its

Talking About ADHD üí¨

What to say when friends or teachers ask questions

How to Explain to Friends

"My brain works a little differently. It's like having a superpower that makes me super creative and full of energy, but sometimes it's hard for me to sit still or wait my turn. It's called ADHD and it's just part of who I am!"

"You know how you can focus easily? My brain is like having 100 TV channels playing at once! It makes me really good at noticing things, but sometimes I need fidget toys or movement breaks to help me focus."

What to Tell Teachers

"I learn better when I can move." Could I stand at my desk sometimes, or sit on a wiggle cushion? It helps my brain focus!

"I sometimes forget things." Can you help me write down homework assignments or give me a checklist? It really helps me remember!

"I'm trying really hard." Even when it looks like I'm not paying attention, I am! My brain just needs to fidget or move to focus better.
